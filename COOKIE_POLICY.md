## 1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how TrustLedger uses cookies and similar tracking
technologies on the Platform. It supplements and is incorporated by reference
into the Privacy Policy and the Terms and Conditions.

---

## 2. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on Your device by Your web browser when You
visit a website. Similar technologies include browser local storage, session
storage, web beacons, and pixels. This Policy refers to all such technologies
collectively as "cookies."

---

## 3. Types of Cookies We Use

**3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies.** These cookies are required for the Platform
to function. They enable core features such as session management, security,
wallet connection state, and authentication. You cannot disable strictly
necessary cookies without preventing the Platform from functioning.

Examples include:

- Session identifiers used to maintain authenticated sessions after magic link
  verification.
- Security tokens used to protect against cross-site request forgery (CSRF).

**3.2 Functional Cookies.** These cookies remember Your preferences and settings
to improve Your experience. They are not strictly necessary but help the
Platform work better for You.

Examples include:

- Dark mode or accessibility preferences.
- Last-connected wallet provider.
- Language and locale preferences.

**3.3 Analytics Cookies.** These cookies collect aggregate information about how
Users interact with the Platform, such as pages visited, features used, and
error rates. Analytics data helps us identify technical issues and improve the
Platform.

Examples include:

- Page-view counters.
- Feature-usage tracking.
- Error-rate monitoring.

TrustLedger's first-party privacy analytics beacon does not use cookies. It is
disabled by default, records only aggregate page-view and frontend-error events
when enabled, strips query strings, and stores no wallet addresses, raw IP
addresses, user-agent strings, emails, documents, session keys, private keys, or
seed phrases.

**3.4 Browser Local Storage.** The Platform uses browser local storage for
certain features, including persisting arbitration draft state
(`src/store/arbitrationDrafts.ts`) and wallet connection preferences. Local
storage is not cleared when You close Your browser tab unless You explicitly
clear it.

---

## 4. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party services integrated into the Platform may set their own cookies,
including wallet-connection software (Reown AppKit), analytics providers, and
infrastructure providers. We do not control third-party cookies. Please review
the privacy policies of those services for information about their cookie
practices.

---

## 5. Cookie Duration

**Session Cookies.** Deleted when You close Your browser.

**Persistent Cookies.** Remain on Your device until they expire or until You
delete them. Expiration periods vary by cookie.

---

## 6. Managing Cookies

**6.1 Browser Settings.** You can control, limit, or delete cookies through Your
browser settings. Most browsers allow You to:

- view cookies stored on Your device;
- delete individual or all cookies; and
- block cookies from specific websites or all websites.

**6.2 Impact of Disabling Cookies.** Disabling strictly necessary cookies may
prevent You from using the Services or cause significant feature degradation.
Disabling functional or analytics cookies may affect Your experience but will
not prevent basic access to the Platform.

**6.3 Local Storage Clearing.** You can clear browser local storage through Your
browser's developer tools or privacy settings.

---

## 7. Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal or Global Privacy Control
(GPC). TrustLedger's first-party privacy analytics collector honors DNT and GPC
signals by not recording analytics events for those requests. Third-party wallet
or infrastructure services may apply their own policies.

---

communicated by updating the "Last Updated" date. Continued use of the Services
after any change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
