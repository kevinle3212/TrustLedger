## 1. Introduction and Scope

**1.1 Who We Are.** TrustLedger is a decentralized escrow, arbitration, and
reputation platform operated by TrustLedger. This Privacy Policy describes how
the Operator collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects information in
connection with the TrustLedger Platform and Services.

**1.2 Scope.** This Policy applies to all users of the TrustLedger Platform,
including the Frontend Application, API routes, email notification services, and
related infrastructure, regardless of how they access the Services. It does not
apply to third-party services or websites linked from the Platform.

**1.3 Acceptance.** By using the Services, You acknowledge that You have read
and understood this Policy and consent to the collection, use, and processing of
Your information as described herein, where consent is the applicable legal
basis.

**1.4 Relationship to Terms.** This Privacy Policy is incorporated by reference
into the Terms and Conditions. Capitalized terms not defined here have the
meanings set forth in the Terms and Conditions.

**1.5 Data Controller.** For purposes of applicable data protection laws,
including the GDPR and UK GDPR, the Operator of the TrustLedger Platform is the
data controller of personal data collected in connection with the Services.
Where the Operator engages third-party service providers to process data on its
behalf, those parties act as data processors subject to contractual data
processing obligations.

**1.6 No Data Protection Officer.** The Operator has not formally designated a
Data Protection Officer (DPO) as of the Effective Date. The Operator has
assessed whether a DPO appointment is required under applicable law. Users may
direct all data protection inquiries to the contact address in Section 16.

## 2. Information We Collect

**2.1 Information You Provide Directly.**

(a) **Email Address.** If You use magic-link authentication or opt in to
notifications, You provide Your email address. We use this to send magic links,
deadline reminders, and service-related communications.

(b) **User Content.** You may submit contract documents, evidence, proof-of-work
URIs, dispute summaries, and other content through the Services. Such content
may be stored on the Platform or on IPFS.

(c) **Feedback and Communications.** If You contact us with questions, feedback,
or bug reports, we retain records of such communications.

**2.2 Information Collected Automatically.**

(a) **Wallet Address.** When You connect Your Wallet to the Services, Your
Wallet address is collected. Wallet addresses are pseudonymous but publicly
visible on the blockchain.

(b) **Transaction Data.** Blockchain Transactions initiated through the Platform
are recorded on-chain and permanently visible. We may also retain metadata about
Transactions for operational purposes.

(c) **Log Data.** Our servers and third-party infrastructure providers may
automatically collect server logs including IP addresses, browser type,
operating system, referring URLs, pages visited, timestamps, and error data.

(d) **Device and Browser Information.** We may collect information about the
device and browser You use to access the Services, including browser version,
screen resolution, language preferences, and device identifiers.

(e) **Usage Analytics.** When privacy analytics are enabled, we may collect
aggregate usage data such as page views and frontend error counts to improve
the Services. TrustLedger's first-party analytics collector is disabled by
default, honors Do Not Track and Global Privacy Control signals, strips query
strings, and does not store wallet addresses, raw IP addresses, raw user-agent
strings, email addresses, raw documents, encrypted draft bodies, session keys,
private keys, or seed phrases.

(f) **Cookies and Local Storage.** See Section 5 for details on cookies and
tracking technologies.

**2.3 Information from Third Parties.**

(a) **Blockchain Data.** We may retrieve publicly available data from blockchain
networks, including transaction history, contract state, token balances, and
wallet activity associated with wallet addresses used with our Services.

(b) **Oracle Data.** We receive exchange rate and market data from third-party
oracle providers.

(c) **Infrastructure Providers.** Our cloud and infrastructure providers may
provide us with operational data about service usage, errors, and performance.

**2.4 Information We Do Not Collect.** We do not collect:

(a) private keys, seed phrases, or wallet passwords (and you must never provide
these to us);

(b) government-issued identification documents, unless we implement a KYC
program;

(c) payment card numbers or banking information; or

(d) sensitive personal information such as racial or ethnic origin, health data,
or biometric data.

## 3. How We Use Information

We use the information collected for the following purposes. For each purpose,
we identify the lawful basis we rely upon under applicable data protection law
(including the GDPR and UK GDPR where applicable). Where multiple bases may
apply, we rely on the most appropriate one for the specific processing activity.

**3.1 Service Delivery.** To operate, provide, and maintain the Services,
including processing Transactions, delivering notifications, managing sessions,
and serving the Frontend Application. _Legal basis: Performance of our agreement
with You (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)); or our legitimate interests in operating the
Platform._

**3.2 Authentication.** To send magic links and verify user sessions. _Legal
basis: Performance of our agreement with You (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b))._

**3.3 Notifications.** To send deadline reminders, service alerts, and other
operational communications related to the Services. _Legal basis: Performance of
our agreement with You (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)); or Your consent where optional
notifications are selected._

**3.4 Security and Fraud Prevention.** To detect, prevent, and respond to fraud,
abuse, security threats, and violations of these Terms. _Legal basis: Our
legitimate interests in protecting the Platform and Users (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f));
or compliance with legal obligations (GDPR Art. 6(1)(c))._

**3.5 Service Improvement.** To analyze usage patterns, identify technical
issues, and improve the Platform's features, performance, and user experience.
_Legal basis: Our legitimate interests in improving and operating the Platform
(GDPR Art. 6(1)(f))._

**3.6 Legal Compliance.** To comply with applicable laws, regulations, legal
processes, and governmental requests, including law enforcement demands and
court orders. _Legal basis: Compliance with legal obligations (GDPR Art.
6(1)(c))._

**3.7 Enforcement.** To enforce our Terms and Conditions, resolve disputes
between Users, and protect our rights and the rights of Users. _Legal basis: Our
legitimate interests in enforcing our legal rights (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f))._

**3.8 Communication.** To respond to inquiries, feedback, and support requests.
_Legal basis: Our legitimate interests in maintaining User communications (GDPR
Art. 6(1)(f)); or performance of our agreement with You._

**3.9 Aggregate Analytics.** To produce anonymized or aggregated analytics that
do not identify individual users. _Legal basis: Our legitimate interests in
understanding Platform usage. Fully anonymized or aggregated data that cannot
identify an individual is not subject to data protection law._

**3.10 Withdrawal of Consent.** Where processing is based on Your consent, You
may withdraw that consent at any time by contacting us at the address in Section
16 or by following the unsubscribe instructions in any communication. Withdrawal
of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before
withdrawal, and does not affect processing conducted on other legal bases.

## 4. Blockchain and Public Data

**4.1 Public Nature of Blockchain Data.** All Transactions submitted to a
blockchain are permanently and publicly visible on the relevant blockchain
network. This includes:

(a) wallet addresses involved in Transactions;

(b) transaction amounts, timestamps, and function calls;

(c) escrow contract terms encoded in the Smart Contracts;

(d) evidence hashes and URIs submitted on-chain;

(e) juror selections, votes, and rulings; and

(f) reputation ratings.

**4.2 No Deletion of On-Chain Data.** The Operator has no ability to delete,
modify, or obscure any data recorded on a public blockchain. Any request to
delete on-chain data cannot be fulfilled. This is an inherent characteristic of
public blockchain technology.

**4.3 IPFS Content.** Content pinned to IPFS is publicly accessible via the IPFS
network and may be cached or republished by any node. Requests to delete IPFS
content cannot be guaranteed to result in complete removal from the IPFS
network.

**4.4 Pseudonymity Limitations.** Wallet addresses are pseudonymous, not
anonymous. Blockchain analytics tools and public records may allow third parties
to associate wallet addresses with real-world identities. The Operator is not
responsible for deanonymization by third parties.

**5.1 Types of Technologies Used.** The Platform may use the following
technologies:

(a) **Essential Cookies.** Cookies necessary for the operation of the Platform,
including session management and security features. These cannot be disabled
without affecting core functionality.

(b) **Functional Cookies.** Cookies that remember Your preferences and settings
to improve Your experience.

(c) **Analytics Technologies.** Technologies that collect aggregate usage data
to help us understand how the Platform is used and how to improve it.

(d) **Local Storage.** Browser local storage may be used to persist wallet
connection state, session information, and user preferences.

**5.2 Third-Party Cookies.** Third-party services integrated into the Platform
(such as analytics providers or wallet connection software) may set their own
cookies or use tracking technologies. We do not control third-party cookies.

**5.3 Cookie Control.** You can control cookies through your browser settings.
Disabling essential cookies may prevent You from using certain features of the
Platform. A separate Cookie Policy may be published in the repository. Until
published, the disclosures in this Section 5 constitute the Operator's full
cookie disclosure. Requests for additional information about specific cookies
used may be directed to the address in Section 16.

## 8. Data Retention

**8.1 General Retention.** We retain non-public personal data for as long as
necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Policy, including to provide
the Services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our
Terms.

**8.2 Email Data.** Email addresses associated with magic-link sessions are
retained for as long as necessary to deliver notifications and for a reasonable
period thereafter for legal and audit purposes. Users may request deletion as
described in Section 11.

**8.3 Log Data.** Server logs and usage analytics may be retained for a limited
period for security and performance purposes, after which they are deleted or
anonymized.

**8.4 Blockchain Data.** Data recorded on a blockchain is permanently retained
on that blockchain and cannot be deleted by the Operator.

**8.5 IPFS Data.** Content pinned to IPFS may persist indefinitely on the IPFS
network regardless of whether the Operator maintains an active pin. The Operator
may discontinue pinning at any time but cannot guarantee content removal from
the network.

**8.6 Legal Hold.** We may retain data beyond normal retention periods if
required by applicable law, ongoing litigation, regulatory inquiry, or law
enforcement request.

**9.4 Breach Notification.** In the event of a security breach that may affect

authorities within the timeframes required by applicable law. Under the GDPR and

state data breach notification laws, notification timelines vary. The Operator
will follow the most stringent applicable requirement.

## 11. Your Rights and Choices

**11.1 Access and Correction.** Depending on Your jurisdiction, You may have the
right to request access to, or correction of, personal data we hold about You.
To make such a request, contact us at the address in Section 16. We will respond
to requests within the timeframes required by applicable law (generally 30 days
under the GDPR; 45 days under the CCPA, subject to a 45-day extension where
reasonably necessary).

**11.2 Deletion (Right to Erasure).** You may request deletion of certain
non-public personal data we hold about You, such as Your email address. We will
honor deletion requests to the extent technically feasible and consistent with
our legal obligations (including data retention obligations). We cannot delete
data recorded on public blockchains or permanently pinned to IPFS. Deletion
requests may be denied where data is required for the performance of a contract,
compliance with a legal obligation, or the establishment, exercise, or defense
of legal claims.

**11.3 Opt-Out of Notifications.** You may opt out of non-essential email
notifications by following the unsubscribe instructions in any email we send or
by contacting us. You cannot opt out of service-essential communications (such
as security notices or legally required disclosures).

**11.4 Cookie Controls.** You can control cookies through your browser settings.
Essential cookies cannot be disabled without affecting core functionality.
Details on the specific cookies used and instructions for controlling them are
available upon request to the address in Section 16.

**11.5 GDPR and UK GDPR Rights.** If You are located in the European Economic
Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, You may have the following rights
under the GDPR, UK GDPR, or equivalent laws:

(a) **Right of Access** — to obtain a copy of Your personal data and information
about how it is processed (Art. 15 GDPR);

(b) **Right to Rectification** — to correct inaccurate personal data (Art. 16);

(c) **Right to Erasure** — to request deletion in certain circumstances (Art.
17);

(d) **Right to Restriction** — to restrict processing in certain circumstances
(Art. 18);

(e) **Right to Portability** — to receive Your data in a structured, commonly
used, machine-readable format, where technically feasible (Art. 20);

(f) **Right to Object** — to object to processing based on legitimate interests
(Art. 21);

(g) **Right to Lodge a Complaint** — You have the right to lodge a complaint
with Your national data protection supervisory authority (Art. 77 GDPR). For EEA
residents, You may contact the supervisory authority in the EU member state
where You reside, work, or where the alleged infringement occurred. For UK
residents, You may contact the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at
ico.org.uk. The Operator's primary establishment for GDPR purposes, if
applicable, is in the United States.

Contact us at the address in Section 16 to exercise any of these rights.

**11.6 CCPA / CPRA Rights.** If You are a California resident, You may have the
following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California
Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

(a) Right to Know — what personal information we collect, use, disclose, and
sell;

(b) Right to Delete — personal information we have collected from You;

(c) Right to Correct — inaccurate personal information;

(d) Right to Opt Out of Sale / Sharing — we do not sell or share personal data
for cross-context behavioral advertising;

(e) Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information — we do not process
sensitive personal information beyond what is necessary to provide Services;

(f) Right to Non-Discrimination — we will not discriminate against You for
exercising any CCPA/CPRA right.

To submit a verifiable consumer request, contact us at the address in
Section 16.

**11.7 Other U.S. State Privacy Rights.** If You reside in Virginia, Colorado,
Connecticut, Texas, or another U.S. state that has enacted a comprehensive

**11.8 Verification.** To protect Your privacy, we may verify Your identity

## 12. Children's Privacy

**12.1 Not for Children.** The Services are not directed to children under the
age of eighteen (18). We do not knowingly collect personal information from
children under the age of eighteen. If we learn that we have collected personal
information from a child under eighteen, we will take steps to delete it
promptly.

**12.2 COPPA.** We do not knowingly collect personal information from children
under the age of thirteen (13) within the meaning of the Children's Online
Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). If You are a parent or guardian and believe that
Your child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us
at the address in Section 16, and we will take steps to delete such information
promptly.

**12.3 Reporting.** If You believe we may have inadvertently collected
information from a child under eighteen, please contact us at the address in
Section 16.

**12.4 Shine the Light (California).** California Civil Code § 1798.83 permits
California residents to request information about third parties to whom the
Operator has disclosed personal information for direct marketing purposes during
the preceding calendar year. The Operator does not disclose personal information
to third parties for direct marketing purposes and therefore does not maintain
records responsive to this section. California residents may contact us at the
address in Section 16 to confirm this policy.
