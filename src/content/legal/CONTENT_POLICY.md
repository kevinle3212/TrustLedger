## 1. Introduction

This Content Policy describes the standards applicable to all Content submitted,
uploaded, or transmitted through the TrustLedger Platform. It supplements and is
incorporated by reference into the Terms and Conditions and the Acceptable Use
Policy.

All capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings in the Terms and
Conditions.

---

## 2. General Standards

All Content submitted through the Services must:

- be truthful and not fraudulent, falsified, or fabricated;
- comply with all applicable laws and regulations;
- not infringe or violate the rights of any third party;
- not cause harm to any person or entity; and
- be consistent with the purpose of the Platform.

---

## 3. Permitted Content

The Platform is designed to facilitate:

- Contract proposals, terms, and related freelance agreement documents;
- Proof-of-work submissions and deliverable documentation;
- Dispute evidence, including factual descriptions, screenshots, links, and
  supporting documentation;
- Juror vote commitments and reveal data;
- Ratings and performance feedback based on genuine firsthand experience;
- Technical documentation and metadata associated with Smart Contract
  interactions; and
- IPFS URIs and cryptographic hashes referencing off-chain documents.

---

## 4. Prohibited Content

The following types of Content are prohibited:

**4.1 Fraudulent Content.** Content designed to deceive, defraud, or mislead
other Users or the Operator, including:

- fabricated or falsified contract documents;
- fake or stolen proof-of-work deliverables;
- false evidence in arbitration proceedings;
- fraudulent escrow proposals with no genuine underlying project;
- counterfeit identity documents or credentials.

**4.2 Illegal Content.** Content that violates applicable law, including:

- child sexual abuse material or any sexualized content involving minors;
- content promoting terrorism, violence, or organized crime;
- content constituting illegal discrimination;
- content that violates applicable export controls or sanctions; or
- content that constitutes criminal activity.

**4.3 Malicious Code.** Files, scripts, or links that contain or distribute
viruses, malware, ransomware, spyware, or other harmful software.

**4.4 Harassment and Abuse.** Content that:

- threatens, harasses, or abuses any individual or group;
- constitutes doxxing (publishing private personal information about
  identifiable individuals without consent);
- is defamatory, false, and damaging to a person's reputation; or
- promotes or incites violence against any person or group.

**4.5 Intellectual Property Infringement.** Content that infringes the
copyright, trademark, patent, or other intellectual property rights of third
parties.

**4.6 Privacy Violations.** Content that discloses another person's private
information without consent, including private communications, financial data,
medical information, government identification numbers, or other sensitive
personal data.

**4.7 Spam and Unsolicited Content.** Repetitive, bulk, or unsolicited Content
submitted for purposes unrelated to the Platform's intended use.

**4.8 Offensive or Harmful Content.** Content that is obscene, pornographic,
hateful, or otherwise grossly offensive without redemptive purpose.

**4.9 Misleading Technical Content.** Incorrect or misleading contract hashes,
proof-of-work URIs, evidence URIs, or other technical metadata submitted with
the intent to deceive other Users or the arbitration system.

## 5. Immutability Notice

**Before submitting any Content to the Platform or to a blockchain:**

- Ensure the Content complies with this Policy.
- Understand that Content submitted on-chain or to IPFS may be permanent,
  immutable, and publicly accessible forever.
- The Operator cannot delete, modify, or retract on-chain or IPFS Content after
  submission.

Think carefully before submitting. You cannot take it back.
