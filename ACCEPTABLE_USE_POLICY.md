## 1. Introduction

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs all use of the TrustLedger Platform
and Services. It supplements and is incorporated by reference into the Terms and
Conditions. Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings in the Terms
and Conditions.

By accessing or using the Services, You agree to comply with this AUP.
Violations may result in immediate suspension or termination of access and may
expose You to civil or criminal liability.

---

## 2. Permitted Uses

You may use the Services to:

- create, propose, accept, fund, manage, and close freelance escrow agreements;
- submit, review, approve, and dispute proof of work;
- participate as a juror in the arbitration system;
- submit and review evidence in arbitration proceedings;
- build and maintain an on-chain reputation;
- interact with the Platform's Smart Contracts on supported blockchain networks;
- access the Platform's APIs for legitimate development and integration purposes
  in accordance with any applicable API terms; and
- explore the open-source codebase in accordance with the Apache License,
  Version 2.0.

All use must be lawful, honest, and consistent with the purpose of the Platform.

## 3. Prohibited Uses — General Conduct

You must not use the Services to:

**3.1 Violate Laws.** Engage in any activity that violates applicable federal,
state, local, international, or foreign law, statute, regulation, or
governmental order, including laws governing financial services, cryptocurrency,
money transmission, anti-money-laundering, sanctions, consumer protection, data
privacy, export control, or employment.

**3.2 Fraud and Deception.** Engage in fraud, deception, misrepresentation,
false advertising, or any scheme designed to obtain money, property, or value
through dishonest means, including but not limited to:

- advance-fee fraud or "419" schemes;
- invoice fraud or payment redirection fraud;
- fake job or contract scams;
- fabricating credentials, qualifications, or work history;
- submitting falsified proof-of-work;
- fabricating or manipulating contract documents; or
- impersonating another User, business, or the Operator.

**3.3 Financial Crime.** Engage in money laundering, terrorist financing,
sanctions evasion, securities fraud, market manipulation, tax evasion, or any
other financial crime.

**3.4 Harassment.** Harass, threaten, intimidate, stalk, bully, defame, abuse,
or otherwise harm any person or group. This includes sending repeated unwanted
communications, making threats of physical or financial harm, and publishing
private information about another person (doxxing).

**3.5 Impersonation.** Impersonate any person, organization, or entity,
including the Operator, its team members, Contributors, other Users, or any
public figure.

**3.6 Intellectual Property Infringement.** Upload, submit, share, or otherwise
use Content that infringes any third party's copyright, trademark, patent, trade
secret, or other intellectual property right.

**3.7 Illegal Content.** Submit, upload, or distribute any Content that is
illegal, including without limitation: content involving the sexual exploitation
or abuse of minors; content inciting violence; content that constitutes illegal
discrimination; or content that is otherwise unlawful in the applicable
jurisdiction.

**3.8 Harmful Content.** Submit, publish, or distribute Content that is
defamatory, obscene, pornographic, hateful, or that promotes or glorifies
violence, illegal activity, or discrimination based on protected
characteristics.

**3.9 Privacy Violations.** Collect, harvest, or disclose the personal
information of other Users without their consent; publish private information
about identifiable individuals; or violate applicable privacy laws.

## 4. Prohibited Uses — Technical and Security

**4.1 Unauthorized Access.** Attempt to gain unauthorized access to the
Services, related systems, or networks, including by bypassing authentication,
exploiting access controls, or using stolen credentials.

**4.2 Malicious Code.** Upload, transmit, or deploy viruses, malware,
ransomware, spyware, adware, trojans, worms, rootkits, logic bombs, cryptomining
code, or any other harmful software.

**4.3 Denial of Service.** Conduct or facilitate denial-of-service (DoS) or
distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks, packet flooding, amplification
attacks, or any activity designed to overload, disrupt, or degrade the Services.

**4.4 Unauthorized Security Testing.** Conduct penetration testing,
vulnerability scanning, fuzzing, exploit development, or security research
against the Operator's hosted infrastructure, live APIs, or production systems
without prior written authorization. Testing of the open-source Smart Contract
code on public testnets is permitted pursuant to the Apache License.

**4.5 Reverse Engineering of Proprietary Components.** Decompile, disassemble,
reverse engineer, or attempt to derive source code from any hosted proprietary
component of the Services that is not available as open-source software, except
as expressly permitted by applicable law.

**4.6 Automated Abuse.** Use bots, scrapers, crawlers, automated scripts, or
other automated means to access or interact with the Services in a manner that
places excessive load on the Platform, harvests data without authorization, or
otherwise abuses automated access privileges.

**4.7 Spam.** Send unsolicited bulk messages, spam, chain letters, or commercial
communications through or in connection with the Services.

**4.8 Credential Theft.** Attempt to obtain another User's Wallet credentials,
seed phrase, private key, magic link, session token, or other authentication
information through any means, including phishing, social engineering, malware,
or technical exploitation.

**4.9 Smart Contract Exploitation.** Exploit, manipulate, or abuse the Smart
Contracts in ways not intended by their design, including reentrancy attacks,
oracle manipulation, juror-selection manipulation, flash loan attacks, sandwich
attacks, front-running, or unauthorized fund extraction.

**4.10 Circumvention of Controls.** Circumvent, disable, or otherwise interfere
with any security feature, rate limit, access control, or platform governance
mechanism.

**4.11 Counterfeit Platform.** Create, deploy, or operate a counterfeit or
fraudulent copy of the Platform, Frontend Application, or Smart Contracts that
impersonates the official TrustLedger Platform.

## 5. Prohibited Uses — Financial and Marketplace

**5.1 Fraudulent Escrow.** Create escrow proposals or contracts with fraudulent
intent, including creating fake projects to extract funds, submitting fake proof
of work, or funding escrows with misrepresented terms.

**5.2 Juror Collusion.** Coordinate with other jurors, parties, or third parties
to predetermine or manipulate dispute outcomes. Submit false evidence to
influence juror decisions. Attempt to bribe, threaten, or coerce any juror.

**5.3 Rating Manipulation.** Submit ratings with the intent to manipulate the
Platform's reputation system, including coordinated rating campaigns,
retaliatory ratings, or false positive ratings exchanged between colluding
parties.

**5.4 Money Laundering.** Use the escrow or payment features of the Platform to
launder proceeds of crime or to disguise the origin of funds.

**5.5 Sanctions Violations.** Use the Services to transfer funds to, or receive
funds from, individuals or entities subject to applicable economic sanctions.

**5.6 Token Manipulation.** Exploit abnormal ERC-20 token behavior to extract
value from escrows or other Users.

**5.7 Self-Dealing and Wash Trading.** Create, fund, or execute escrow contracts
or other interactions with wallets You own or control, or with parties acting in
coordination with You, for the purpose of: (a) artificially inflating Your
reputation score or that of any other User; (b) generating false transaction
history to appear as an active or experienced User; (c) creating fraudulent
evidence of completed work; or (d) manipulating the Platform's reputation,
analytics, or ranking systems. This prohibition applies regardless of whether
the underlying transactions are economically real.

**5.8 AI-Generated Abuse.** Use AI tools, large language models, generative AI
systems, automated content generation, or deepfake technology to: (a) fabricate,
falsify, or misrepresent evidence submitted in any arbitration proceeding; (b)
generate fraudulent proof-of-work submissions; (c) create fake identities,
impersonation profiles, or deceptive personas on the Platform; (d) produce bulk
fraudulent communications, fraudulent contract documents, or synthetic
documentation; or (e) otherwise abuse or deceive the Platform's systems or other
Users through AI-generated content.

**5.9 Facilitation of Prohibited Activity.** Assist, enable, encourage, or
facilitate any other User or third party in engaging in any activity prohibited
by this AUP or the Terms and Conditions, including by providing tools, code,
scripts, credentials, instructions, or funding for such activities.

**5.10 False Reporting.** Submit knowingly false reports of violations, false
DMCA notices, false evidence submissions, or false reports of abusive conduct
against other Users. Filing false reports may expose You to civil liability to
the falsely accused party and to termination of Your access.

Content or activity, and the absence of enforcement action in any particular
instance does not constitute a waiver of the right to enforce this AUP in other
instances.

**6.5 No Liability for Enforcement.** The Operator shall not be liable to You or
any third party for any enforcement action taken in good faith pursuant to this
AUP.
