## 1. Introduction

TrustLedger respects intellectual property rights and complies with the Digital
Millennium Copyright Act ("DMCA"), 17 U.S.C. § 512 et seq. This policy describes
the procedures for submitting copyright infringement notices and
counter-notices. It is incorporated by reference into the Terms and Conditions.

---

## 2. Reporting Copyright Infringement

If You believe that Content accessible through the Services infringes Your
copyright, submit a written notification containing all of the following
elements to the address in Section 6:

**2.1 Required Elements.** Your DMCA notice must include:

(a) **Identification of the Work.** A description of the copyrighted work You
claim has been infringed, or if multiple works are covered by a single notice, a
representative list of such works.

(b) **Identification of the Infringing Material.** A description of the material
You claim is infringing and information reasonably sufficient to enable the
Operator to locate it on the Services (e.g., the specific URL, Smart Contract
address, IPFS CID, or other identifying information).

(c) **Your Contact Information.** Your name, mailing address, telephone number,
and email address.

(d) **Good-Faith Belief Statement.** A statement that You have a good-faith
belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by
the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

(e) **Accuracy Statement.** A statement that the information in the notification
is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that You are authorized to act on
behalf of the copyright owner.

(f) **Signature.** Your physical or electronic signature.

Notices missing any required element may not be processed.

---

## 3. What Happens After We Receive a Notice

Upon receipt of a valid and complete DMCA notice, the Operator will:

(a) promptly review the notice for completeness and validity;

(b) disable access to or remove the allegedly infringing material from the
Frontend Application to the extent technically feasible;

(c) notify the User who submitted the affected Content, if technically feasible;
and

(d) terminate access to the Services for repeat infringers, in appropriate
circumstances.

**Limitation Regarding Immutable Content.** The Operator cannot delete, alter,
or remove Content recorded on a public blockchain or permanently pinned to a
decentralized storage network such as IPFS. Removal from the Frontend
Application is the maximum remedy available to the Operator for such content.

---

## 4. Counter-Notice Procedure

If Your Content was removed or disabled in response to a DMCA notice that You
believe was submitted in error or on the basis of a misidentification, You may
submit a counter-notice. Your counter-notice must comply with 17 U.S.C. §
512(g)(3) and must include:

(a) your physical or electronic signature;

(b) identification of the material that has been removed and the location at
which the material appeared before it was removed;

(c) a statement under penalty of perjury that You have a good-faith belief that
the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or
misidentification;

(d) your name, address, and telephone number; and

(e) a statement that You consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District
Court for the judicial district in which Your address is located (or if Your
address is outside the United States, the judicial district in which the
Operator is located), and that You will accept service of process from the
person who submitted the original DMCA notice.

Upon receipt of a valid counter-notice, the Operator will provide the original
noticing party with a copy of the counter-notice and will restore the removed
material within ten (10) to fourteen (14) business days unless the Operator
receives notice that the original noticing party has filed an action seeking a
court order to restrain the re-posting.

## 5. Repeat Infringer Policy

The Operator maintains a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances,
the accounts or access of Users who repeatedly infringe the copyrights or other
intellectual property rights of others.

---

## 6. False Notices

Knowingly submitting a materially false DMCA notice or counter-notice may result
in civil or criminal liability under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f) and applicable law. The
Operator reserves the right to seek damages, costs, and attorneys' fees against
any party who submits a knowingly false notice.
